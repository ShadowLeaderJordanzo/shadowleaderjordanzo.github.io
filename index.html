How to Change a Tire

Changing a tire is an ageless skill taught from generation to generation, but as of late the reliance on highly paid insurance and calling friends may have left somebody without the general knowledge of changing a tire. It isn’t that high school or college has failed those that lack this skill, it just has become more less and less needed as society developed, and the internet exists as the heart of the youth. This set of instructions exists to find the youth and teach them about handling an emergency on the road – the dreaded flat tire.

Instructions

  1. Park the car.
    1. Engage the parking brake.
    2. Turn the car off.
      Note

      If you are on the road, pull over to a safe location on the shoulder.

    3. If you have bricks or wood, place them in front and behind the wheel you are replacing.
  2. Take out the replacement tire (Tool 3)
  3. Jacking the car up
    1. Take your jack and jacking tool.
    2. Find the jacking point. (Image 1)
    3. Attach the Jack Extension (Tool 1a) to your Jack (Tool 1) and either twist it or pump it.
      Note

      Scissor Jacks typically have twist, while normal jacks pump. So, depending on the jack, you may have to pump or twist.

    4. Once the tire is one foot off the ground stop lifting the car or until your jack can’t lift anymore. (Image 2)
  4. Replacing the wheel.
    1. Take out the Lug Wrench (Tool 2).
    2. Twist the lugs on the hub to the left until they are loose and can be pulled off. (Image 2a)
    3. Pull the tire off.
      It should come right off.
    4. Take out the replacement tire (Tool 3).
    5. Place the old tire in the trunk.
    6. Place the replacement tire on the hub. (Image 2b)
    7. Tighten (twist to the right) the lug nuts to secure the replacement tire onto the hub.
      Be sure that after you do it to all of them you do it again and make sure they are as tight as possible.
  5. Bringing the car down.
    1. Twist in the opposite direction from before to lower the car. If you pumped before twist the Jack Extension to the left and it will release.
      Note

      Sometimes there is a lever you must press, refer to your jack’s manual or online if you are having trouble. (Image 3)
